Architecture

Functional Architecture

There are 4 main subsystems involved in the operation of the Phlebot system: Perception, Planning/Control and Actuation. The perception system runs a neural network on an NVIDIA Jetson Nano. The network takes input images from a RGB MIPI camera and segments the image to detect veins. The planning/control subsystem takes these classifications and uses them to determine where to move to next and whether to enter the next phase of operation. The actuation subsytem controls the various pneumatics and steppers used within the system.

Electronics Architecture

The electronics architecute shows the connections between the various components to transfer power and data. All componentsrely on a central 12 V power supply. This feeds directly into the Stepper and solenoid drivers, while the Jetson gets this power after it is fed through a 5V buck-converter to drop to an appropriate operating voltage. The three stepper motors are connected to the 3D printer driver board, a Big Tree Tech SKR Pico, using standard MOLEC connectors. The Jetson serves as the main computer and communicates with the peripheral Arduino and printer control board using Serial over USB.
